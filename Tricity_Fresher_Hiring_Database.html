Scope note — read this before relying on the numbers

The original brief asked for 100 agencies, 200 companies, 300 named recruiters, live June-2026 job postings with hiring-manager names, and Google-Maps-optimized routes with travel times. After testing, several of these targets aren't achievable without fabricating data:

  • Phone/WhatsApp numbers, named contacts, ratings for 100 agencies: most agencies don't publish this. What's in 01 is everything verifiable today, with source URLs.
  • HR/recruitment emails for 200 companies: almost none are publicly listed; companies route hiring through ATS portals (linked in the Careers Page column).
  • 300 named recruiters with LinkedIn URLs: building this as a static scrape is a mass people-search exercise we won't do. The toolkit in 04 lets each person generate this themselves, which also gets better (personalized) results.
  • Live job postings with deadlines/hiring managers: these change hourly. 03 instead gives live search links per role/portal that always show current results.
  • Maps travel times: 05 sequences real addresses by geographic cluster; exact times need a live Maps query on the day of travel.

Paid Training & "Placement Guarantee" Programs — Honest Assessment

You asked about options that charge a fee, since speed matters. Here's the real landscape, found via search today, plus a framework so the team doesn't lose money to a bad one.

The core rule first

Real recruitment agencies and real employers never charge candidates a fee — they're paid by the hiring company. Anything calling itself a "recruitment agency" or "placement consultancy" that asks you for money before/for a job offer is not operating like a normal agency, full stop.

What's legitimately fee-based in this market is training-with-placement-assistance — you pay for a skills course (testing, MERN/MEAN stack, cloud, etc.), and the institute helps you apply afterward. This can be worth it if: - The fee is for actual instruction/curriculum (verifiable syllabus, instructors, duration), not a vague "registration fee." - "Placement assistance" is explicitly NOT a guaranteed job — read the fine print. "100% placement guarantee" claims should be treated with skepticism; ask for proof: names of recent batches, LinkedIn profiles of people who got placed, which companies hired them and when. - You can verify the institute has a real office (visit it) and has been operating for years (check Google reviews, Glassdoor, MCA/company registration via zaubacorp.com).

Before paying anyone, anything — checklist

  1. Get the fee structure in writing (what exactly does it cover, refund policy if no placement).
  2. Ask for 3-5 recent alumni you can actually message on LinkedIn — message them yourself, don't just trust testimonials on the institute's site.
  3. Check company registration on zaubacorp.com or mca.gov.in — confirms it's a real registered entity, not a fly-by-night operation.
  4. Visit the physical office before paying — addresses above are a starting point.
  5. Never pay via UPI/cash to an individual's personal account — payment should go to the registered company's bank account/official payment gateway, with a proper invoice/receipt.
  6. Compare the fee to the 30-day free plan in 08_30day_execution_plan.md — six of you doing the free plan consistently for 4-6 weeks may outperform a paid course, especially since you already have B.Tech CSE (AI/ML) fundamentals. Paid training makes most sense for a specific skill gap (e.g., one of you needs Power BI or a cloud cert), not as a blanket "guaranteed job" purchase.
